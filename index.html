Service 1 of 5 · Lead Gen

Google Ads

What it is
We rank their company at the top of Google and bring in about 10 extra details a week. No money up front, fully performance based.
What to tell the client
"We put you at the #1 spot on Google. You only pay for qualified leads, real buying-intent calls, and it's just 10% of your basic detail."
Price
10% of their interior/exterior detail per qualified lead. A $250 detail = $25 per lead, flat no matter the job size.
Your commission
Setter 40%, Closer 25% of the first bi-weekly invoice (no setter, closer gets 40%).
~$500 invoice: Setter $200, Closer $125.

How To Pitch It · Setting The Call

The setting flow

1
Open with the benefit: "Can you take on about 10 more details a week?"
2
Find the want: "Are you looking to bring on more work?"
3
Quick discovery: current volume, capacity, do they do ceramics?
4
Pitch it simple, in their language: jobs, not jargon
5
Stay transparent, differentiate, fold in what they already have
6
Scarcity, then book the call
Talk track

The setter's only job is to book a qualified call. Lead with more jobs, keep it simple, and pin a specific time. Closing happens on the next call.

The Pitch · Word For Word

Say it like this

The hook
"I bring detailers more jobs. About 8 to 15 details a week, plus around 10 ceramic coatings a month."
The price, simple
"No money up front. We cover ad spend and get you ranked #1 on Google. We just take 10% of your detail per qualified lead. A $200 detail, that's $20."
The "what's the catch"
"The catch is we keep that 10%. That's it. We only charge for real buying-intent leads, not price-shoppers."
The "why so cheap" + scarcity
"Low turnover. We grow with you, and we have other services, so when we make you money you reinvest. And we only work with one detailer per major city, since there's only one #1 on Google."
Talk track

If they already run Facebook or have Google reviews, fold it in: "Perfect, we connect your reviews and put our ads behind you." Never fight what they have, build on it.

Close The Set

Lock the call

1
"Let's hop on a quick Zoom to go over everything and see if it's a fit."
2
Offer a specific time: "Free tomorrow morning or evening? I've got 6 PM."
3
Pin it, confirm the timezone, get their email for the invite
4
Grab their website / details so the closer is prepped

A set isn't a set until a specific time is on the calendar and you have their email.

Talk track

Never leave it at "sometime tomorrow." Pin the exact hour, confirm the timezone, and send the invite while you're still on the phone.
